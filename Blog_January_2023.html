{< >Sweet Dreams are made of these </>}

It's been a minute since my last post! I did't realize almost a month has gone by since the first opening. We are well underway into 2023 at this point and I am still at the relative front steps of this journey. However I used to look at how far I have to go and the fear and doubt would well up. however I can now look back and that I have been introduced to a lot so far. There are a few things that I can definitely see have changed. No I not coding like the hackers from movies. (nobody does really) Just like reading and writing a human language it takes time and practice. I can report that both variables have paid off my investments in them. CSS is deep and broad bro! I am focusing on how it works rather than trying to learn every little thing right now. I initially projected with time I would be on to Java right now. I have cheated and looked ahead only to realize I should fall back and let it "do what it do" so to speak. I have a supplemental course I purchased on sale at Udemy as the instructor is actually as excellent as the reviews suggested I came to find out. At the moment we are going over CSS Selectors and specificity. Not a very sexy but essential topic. much of what I am doing now is in a way review. however there is a significant difference between complete self study and getting the help and guidance of experienced and knowledgeable instructors. One of the biggest changes has been my comfort level with various tools. As the comfort level increases my willingness to experiment and explore increases as I become more confident with my moves. Another big improvement is learning about reliable resources like the Mozilla Developer Network or MDN These have helped me reference things as I needed them and help me discover more. However at the moment of this writing none of these resources are available. Why? The internet is down. I have been hearing about it every since it happened. One thing I found amazing was how close the kids could detect the network was up as opposed to a continues ping. I had one setup when I initially started troubleshooting the network. I explained to my daughter that the likelihood the problem was internal was low. The explanation I gave her was that the administrator in charge of her network was excellent at his job and normally problems will be traced to an external source. Moments later a Rogers utility van parks on the corner. some time went by and the van eventually left. Looking at the ping results suggested the network was still heavily struggling. I starred at the black screen. Long column of "Request timed out" mocked me from behind the blackness. I suddenly heard my daughter exclaim "its back up!" I looked back at my screen and the next few lines scrolled by reading:

"Reply from 8.8.8.8: bytes=32 time=91ms TTL=119"

"Reply from 8.8.8.8: bytes=32 time=91ms TTL=118"

"Reply from 8.8.8.8: bytes=32 time=91ms TTL=108"


She must have sensed it or something. It's since been hit and miss. Mostly miss. I continue to tinker in my local Dev environment. I did get a chance to finish one project. A very simple one but cool none the less. The idea is to create a button that seems to levitate slightly when hovered. I noticed others posting the actual snippet from CodePen so naturally I had to learn how then incorporate it.
